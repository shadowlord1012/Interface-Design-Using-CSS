German Heavy tank Pzkpfw. VI Tiger ausf. H1

The Tiger I is an iconic tank of World War II. It became one of the most significant and successful tanks of the war. The history of the Tiger I dates back to 1942 when it was developed as a response to Germany's need to enhance its defensive capabilities against formidable enemy tanks, particularly the Soviet KV-1 and T-34 tanks. The Tiger I was characterized by its high firepower and strong armor, enabling it to survive on the battlefield against Allied tanks. Additionally, it featured a highly accurate and effective 88mm Kw.K.36 L/56 cannon, allowing it to destroy enemy tanks with great efficiency at long distances.

Swedish Stridsvagn 103C

Stridsvagn 103c, also known as S-Tank, was a Swedish tank destroyer developed and produced during the Cold War (1967 - 1971). Strv 103C had a very low profile, providing it with the advantage of surprise and better camouflage. Its armament consisted of a 105mm main gun Bofors L/62, which was highly effective against enemy armored targets.
